About Elephant

Elephant (Loxodonta africana or Elephas maximus): Elephants are the largest terrestrial animals on Earth and are characterized by their massive size, long trunk, and distinctive ivory tusks (in African elephants). They are highly intelligent and social creatures that form close-knit family groups. Elephants are herbivores, primarily feeding on a variety of vegetation, including grasses, leaves, bark, and fruits. They require large amounts of food and water daily to sustain their enormous bodies. Unfortunately, elephants face significant conservation challenges due to habitat loss and poaching for their ivory, making them a focus of conservation efforts in many zoos.
