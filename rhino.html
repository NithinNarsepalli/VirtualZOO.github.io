About Rhinoceros

Rhinoceros (Various species): Rhinoceroses are large herbivorous mammals found in Africa and Asia. Different species, such as the white rhinoceros, black rhinoceros, and Indian rhinoceros, have varying dietary preferences, but they all primarily consume plant materials. Rhinos are known for their thick skin and distinctive horns, which are made of keratin, the same material as human hair and nails. Despite their formidable appearance, rhinos are endangered due to habitat loss and poaching for their horns,and many zoos work to raise awareness and support conservation efforts.
