About Kangaroo

Kangaroo (Macropus spp.): Kangaroos are marsupials native to Australia and nearby regions. They are herbivores, primarily feeding on a diet of grasses and other vegetation. Kangaroos are known for their powerful hind legs, which they use for hopping, and their pouches where they carry and nurse their young, called joeys. They are adapted to a hop-based mode of locomotion that allows them to cover large distances efficiently. Kangaroos are a symbol of Australia and are often featured in zoos worldwide due to their unique evolutionary traits
